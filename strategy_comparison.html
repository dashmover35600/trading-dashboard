Strategy Verdict
What the data tells us

Across five key metrics — win rate, Sharpe, profit factor, expectancy, and avg loss — v18 is the stronger strategy. Gap & Go shows a structural edge in one area: when gaps do resolve in direction, the dollar payoff is 7.5× larger per winner than v18 ($34.90 vs $4.65). But with only a 20.6% win rate, the math doesn't close — negative expectancy means the strategy loses ground on average each trade.

🎯
v18 wins on consistency
73.3% win rate across 101 trades over a full year of hybrid data. Nine confirmation filters (VWAP, EMA, RSI, day bias, volume, momentum, prime window) work together to select only high-probability setups. Low average loss ($13.12) limits damage on losers.
⚡
Gap & Go wins on size
Average winner of $34.90 — 7.5× larger than v18 — because the 2:1 fixed R:R means wins are always twice the gap size. Smaller drawdown (1.91% vs 3.38%) and positive total P&L ($111.01) in a volatile 60-day window including the April 2026 market dislocation.
🔬
Best use case for each
v18: High-frequency intraday trading, daily income generation, pairs well with tight risk controls. Proven over 300 trading days.

Gap & Go: High-volatility event days (earnings reactions, macro shocks) where gaps tend to continue. Consider pairing with a gap-fill filter for better selectivity.
